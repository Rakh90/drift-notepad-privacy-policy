Privacy Policy for Drift Notepad

Last updated: July 21, 2026

Drift Notepad is a notes app built to keep everything on your device. This page explains, plainly, what that means for your data.

The short version

Drift Notepad does not collect, transmit, or share any of your data. There is no account, no server, no analytics, and no advertising in this app. Everything you create — notes, photos, voice memos, tables, categories, templates, and reminders — is stored only in the app's local storage on your own device.

What the app stores locally

All of the following stays on your device and is never sent anywhere:

  • Note text, formatting, checklists, and tables you create
  • Photos you attach to a note
  • Voice memos you record within a note
  • Categories, templates, and app settings, including an optional PIN used to lock individual notes
  • Reminders (timers and alarms) you set on a note

Permissions the app asks for, and why

  • Microphone — only used while you're actively recording a voice memo inside a note. The recording is saved locally on your device; it is never uploaded or transmitted anywhere.
  • Notifications — used to alert you when a reminder (timer or alarm) you set on a note goes off. Notification scheduling happens entirely on your device via Android's own notification system.
  • Photos / files access — used only when you choose to attach a photo to a note, or export/import a backup file. The app never scans or accesses files you haven't explicitly picked.

Data sharing

Drift Notepad shares no data with any third party, because it does not transmit data anywhere in the first place. There are no analytics SDKs, no ad networks, and no crash-reporting services built into the app.

Exporting and sharing your own notes

The app lets you export a backup of your notes (or a single note) to a file, which you can save to your device or share using Android's own share sheet — for example, to send a note to a friend, or move a backup to another device. That sharing is a deliberate action you take, using the file-sharing tools already built into your phone; the app itself does not have a server to receive or store that data.

Deleting your data

You're always in full control:

  • Delete an individual note any time — it goes to Trash and is permanently removed after 30 days, or you can empty Trash immediately.
  • Uninstalling the app removes all of its local data from your device immediately.

Children's privacy

Drift Notepad does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children, because it does not collect information from anyone at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes — for example, if a future version of the app adds a feature that does involve a network request — this page will be updated first, with a new "Last updated" date above.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to the developer, Rakh Studio, via the contact details listed on the Drift Notepad Google Play Store listing.
